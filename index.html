📌 Key Clinical Rules — Quick Reference

Triple WhammyNSAID + ACEi/ARB + Diuretic → ↑ AKI risk — avoid combination
Denosumab — Never Stop AbruptlyMust transition to bisphosphonate immediately to prevent rebound fractures
CS Never for Maintenance (IBD)Corticosteroids are for induction only — not relapse prevention
MTX — Once Weekly OnlyDaily dosing is potentially fatal. Always prescribe with folic acid on a different day.
Thiopurine — TPMT FirstCheck TPMT/NUDT15 genotype before azathioprine or mercaptopurine to prevent myelosuppression
Bisphosphonate Dosing (Oral)Empty stomach, full glass water, remain upright 30 min — or oesophageal toxicity
Acitretin TeratogenicityNo pregnancy for 3 years after stopping. No alcohol during/2 months after.
Zoledronic Acid (Pre-check)Check eGFR >35, Ca normal, 25-OHD >50, hydration before EVERY dose
Hyperkalaemia OrderCa gluconate (membrane) → insulin+glucose/salbutamol (shift) → resonium/dialysis (remove)
ASUC Definition≥6 bloody stools/day + ≥1 of: fever, tachycardia, Hb <105, ESR >30 → admit
Gout SUA Target<0.36 mmol/L (<0.30 mmol/L if tophaceous). Start ULT after 2+ attacks.
IL-17 Inhibitors & IBDSecukinumab/ixekizumab — caution/avoid in Crohn or UC comorbidity
